Week5: Recombination

  1. Linkage Disequilibrium

    If alleles at two sites are independent(on different chromosome): pAB=pApB

    If not! Disequilibrium coefficient:

    (32)D=pAB−pApB

    Note: pA,pB kept unchange while pAB changed with r

    2-locus Wahlund effect: Even if two populations are each in linkage equilibrium, their union is in LD if the allele frequencies between populations differ.(No more in equilibrium when groups are mixed)

    Normalised D:

    (33)D<0:D′=Dmin(pApB,papb)D>0:D′=Dmin(pApb,papB)

    D’ = 1 whenever one allele at SNP1 only exists together with particular allele at SNP2 like following:

    Pearson Correlation(R)

    (34)R=E(AB)−E(A)E(B)Var(A)Var(B)=DpapbpApBR=0(D=0),A,B are independentR=1,A,B are perfectly aligned

  2. Recombination Fraction r

    r=rAB​ is the fraction of the gametes that are recombined with respect to the sites A and B

    r=0.5 if A, B are independent(in separate chromosomes come from different parents), and approach to 0.5 with physical distance grows when they are in the same chromosomes.

    It can be interpreted as the chance of recombination to two sites.

    LD has exp decay with generation: D′=(1−r)D, when r is small(~0) the LD decay very slowly, vice versa.

    Two loci from same chromosome to different: 1/r, from different to the same: 1/2N
